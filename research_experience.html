Research Experience

Personalized Neural ODE Modeling for Alzheimer's Disease

Supervisor: Prof. Wenrui Hao (Penn State University)
Duration: Jan 2025 – Current

In this project, I developed and implemented a novel Neural ODE modeling framework to capture the dynamic progression of AD biomarkers, as well as provide a platform for personalized prediction by fine-tuning key parameters for individual subjects. My architecture balanced model complexity with biological interpretability. This work achieves higher accuracy than traditional models and reveals clearer cascade disease trends.

Quantitative Modeling for Autoimmune & Cardiovascular Diseases

Supervisor: Dr. Cuiping Pan (HKUST(GZ))
Duration: Oct 2025 – Current

Working in Dr. Cuiping Pan’s lab at HKUST(GZ), I am expanding my ODE-based methods to model multimodal data for complex conditions like IgG4-RD and coronary heart disease. Meanwhile, I am developing an in-silico simulation model for drug progression. This effort is providing the first quantitative platform for a previously qualitative-only disease (IgG4-RD) and is helping identify optimal therapeutic targets, further deepening my interest in multimodal medical data.

scTimelyGPT: Transformer Pre-training for Single-cell Transcriptomics Time-Series Forecasting

Supervisors: Dr. Ziyang Song (University of Ohio) & Prof. Yue Li (McGill University)
Duration: Oct 2025 – Current

I am actively collaborating to develop specialized recurrent-memory-Transformer-units GPT models, focusing on single-cell transcriptomics data. We are currently attempting to specialize this for single-cell transcriptomics data to train a foundation model. This work involves developing TimelyGPT Models and conducting experiments for Single-Cell Transcriptomics time series data interpolation and extrapolation, as well as comparing the scTimelyGPT model with generative models such as scNODE.

Research Intern: Tensor Neural Networks and Acceleration Algorithms

Supervisor: Asst. Prof. Zecheng Gan (HKUST (GZ))
Duration: Feb 2025 – June 2025

As a research intern, my responsibilities included:

  • Developing Tensor Neural Networks to solve high-dimensional partial differential equations (PDEs)
  • Mathematically deriving algorithms for Convolutional Tensor Neural Networks (CTNNs) with coupled input dimensions
  • Applying Deep Galerkin/Ritz Methods for Numerical PDEs
  • Profiling and Accelerating training process
